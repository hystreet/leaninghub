The Sellers' Process

In this guide you’ll find instructions for:

  1. Booking a valuation
  2. instructing to sell
  3. getting to market
  4. managing availability
  5. Feedback and offers
  6. Finalising the Sale

Booking a Valuation

In the centre of the consumer site homepage, you’ll see an input field and a button that says: “book your free valuation”.

This is the caption for the image

Enter the postcode of the property you intend to sell and hit the button to start the selling process. If the entered postcode is covered by an agent, you’ll see a page introducing the agent. Go here to find out where the agent image, bio and experience sections come from. If the entered area doesn’t have an agent tied to it, you’ll see a message letting you know.

Each step of the process for booking a valuation gathers the important property information (i.e. the location, property type, expected valuation) and seller contact details. For each section, fill in the fields then click the button at the bottom of the page to move to the next section.

This is the caption for the image

The last step will show you a table of available time slots for to choose from. Once the valuation time has been picked, you’ll be taken to a confirmation page. You can amend or cancel the valuation time at any point.

This is the caption for the image

At the bottom of this page you’ll see a button to take you back to your account page. There is also an Account button at the top right of the screen to take you there- you’ll see this button from on anywhere on the site.

Instructing to Sell

from the account page, click “manage my sale”. This will lead you to a page showing all the steps involved in selling a property.

Once an agent has been to the property and completed a valuation, you’ll be prompted to instruct the agent to start selling the property. Click the button to make the instruction.

This is the caption for the image

The following page will allow you to choose which addons to include in the instruction (prices for these can be set through the admin section). Check the box to accept the terms and conditions then hit continue at the bottom of the page. Next, you’ll see the fields to enter card details for paying for the instruction.

Getting to Market

This stage covers everything needed from the consumer leading up to posting the property listing online. Whilst this section is being completed by the consumer, the agent would normally be creating the listing on the staff side.

book a launch:

after payment has been received, the next step is to arrange a launch. To book a launch, click the button on your account page- this will lead you to a table showing the agents availability (similar to the table seen when booking a valuation). Check the time slot that best suits you, and then click ‘book property launch’. You’ll be given some tips on how to best prepare for the launch, followed by a confirmation page. As with booking a valuation, the appointment can be amended or cancelled at any time.

ID Check:

This section of the “getting to market” checklist is a legal requirement. The first page you see after clicking complete ID check should be read carefully. Once you’ve read through everything, check the box to show that you understand what the ID check entails, the hit ‘let’s start the ID check’.

Fill out all the information on the following pages. Once everything has been filled out, the information will show on the staff site. The Experian check can be carried out from there.

Property facts:

The fact find is also a legal requirement. As with the ID check, read the important information and check the box to confirm that you are completing the fact find as accurately as possible (to the best of your knowledge). Next, hit continue and fill out the form.

Managing Availability

when the property is listed, potential buyers will book viewings against either the agent’s availability, the seller’s availability, or both availabilities at the same time. To Find out more about how availabilities work, go here.

If the seller is going to be present for the viewings (either alone or accompanied by an agent), they need to keep their availability updated so buyers have an accurate set of time slots to book viewings off. To do this go to ‘manage my availability’ from the account page.

This is the caption for the image

From here, you’ll see a similar table to the one from booking a valuation. This time, simply check all the time slots that are available for viewings. buyers will see these times when following the consumer site ‘book a viewing’ process

Feedback and Offers

once viewings have been carried out, buyers are prompted to leave feedback on the property. More information on how buyers leave feedback can be found here. Vendors are able to view feedback either by clicking ‘view feedback’ on from the step four section of the account page, or by going to feedback on the navigation bar at the top of the page.

This is the caption for the image

From the feedback page, you’ll see a box for each buyer that has left feedback. Clicking on the box will show you how the buyer rated the property.

This is the caption for the image

Viewing offers can also be accessed from both the step four section, or the navigation bar. This section will show any new offers that have been made on the property. You have the option to accept or reject and offers here, along with the opportunity to send a message along with your response. Any past offers will also show on this page, regardless of whether you rejected them, or if the buyer withdrew the offer.
