The Sellers' Process

In this guide you’ll find instructions for:

  1. Booking a valuation
  2. instructing to sell
  3. getting to market
  4. managing availability
  5. Feedback and offers
  6. Finalising the Sale

Booking a Valuation

In the centre of the consumer site homepage, you’ll see an input field and a button that says: “book your free valuation”.

This is where seller's will enter their postcodes

To start the selling process, your seller will just need to enter their postcode and hit 'book a free valuation'. If the entered postcode is covered by an agent, they’ll see a page introducing the agent. If the entered area doesn’t have an agent tied to it, they’ll see a message with your branch's contact number to arrange a valuation over the phone.

Each step of the process for booking a valuation gathers some standard pre-valuation information (i.e. full address, property type, and estimated value). For each section, fill in the fields then click the button at the bottom of the page to move to the next section.

If this is the first time the seller has visited your Hystreet consumer site, The next step will ask them to enter to required details to create an account. They will need to enter their name, email address, password and contact number. If an account has already been added using the entered email address, the client will be notified when they try to move on to the next step. In this case, they will want to hit 'login' on your homepage before they try and book the valuation.

TIP: as part of Hystreet's changes for GDPR, your clients will also be asked to choose when they would like to be contacted. Once logged in, they will also be albe to update these preferences by going to account > preferences .

To opt-in, your seller will just need to check their prefered options

The last step will show you a table of available time slots for to choose from. Once the valuation time has been picked, the seller will be taken to a confirmation page. You can amend or cancel the valuation time at any point.

Sellers will be shown this page when their valuation is booked

At the bottom of this page you’ll see a button to take you back to your account page. There is also an Account button at the top right of the screen to take you there- you’ll see this button from on anywhere on the site.

Instructing to Sell

from the account page, click “manage my sale”. This will lead you to a page showing all the steps involved in selling a property.

Once an agent has been to the property and completed a valuation, you’ll be prompted to instruct the agent to start selling the property. Click the button to make the instruction.

Sellers click this to instruct an agent to sell their property

The following page will allow you to choose which addons to include in the instruction (prices for these can be set through the admin section). Check the box to accept the terms and conditions then hit continue at the bottom of the page. Next, you’ll see the fields to enter card details for paying for the instruction.

This is where payment details are recorded

TIP: if you’re an agent doing a trial run of the consumer site, make sure you are using the test stripe keys and card details. Read our guide on setting up Stripe to find out how to do this.

Getting to Market

This stage covers everything needed from the consumer leading up to posting the property listing online. Whilst this section is being completed by the consumer, the agent would normally be creating the listing on the staff side.

book a take on:

after payment has been received, the next step is to arrange a take on. To book a take on, click the button on your account page- this will lead you to a table showing the agents availability (similar to the table seen when booking a valuation). Check the time slot that best suits you, and then click ‘book property take on’. You’ll be given some tips on how to best prepare for the take on, followed by a confirmation page. As with booking a valuation, the appointment can be amended or cancelled at any time.

ID Check:

This section of the “getting to market” checklist is a legal requirement. The first page you see after clicking complete ID check should be read carefully. Once you’ve read through everything, check the box to show that you understand what the ID check entails, the hit ‘let’s start the ID check’.

Fill out all the information on the following pages. Once everything has been filled out, the information will show on the staff site. The Experian check can be carried out from there.

Property facts:

The fact find is also a legal requirement. As with the ID check, read the important information and check the box to confirm that you are completing the fact find as accurately as possible (to the best of your knowledge). Next, hit continue and fill out the form.

Managing Availability

when the property is listed, potential buyers will book viewings against either the agent’s availability, the seller’s availability, or both availabilities at the same time. Our guide on appointments covers how these availabilties work.

If the seller is going to be present for the viewings (either alone or accompanied by an agent), they need to keep their availability updated so buyers have an accurate set of time slots to book viewings off. To do this go to ‘manage my availability’ from the account page.

this is how clients navigate around the consumer site

From here, you’ll see a similar table to the one from booking a valuation. This time, simply check all the time slots that are available for viewings. buyers will see these times when following the consumer site ‘book a viewing’ process

Feedback and Offers

once viewings have been carried out, buyers are prompted to leave feedback on the property. More information on how buyers leave feedback can be found here. Vendors are able to view feedback either by clicking ‘see feedback’ on from the step four section of the account page, or by going to feedback on the navigation bar at the top of the page.

This is how clients wil get to thir feedback

From the feedback page, you’ll see a box for each buyer that has left feedback. Clicking on the box will show you how the buyer rated the property.

This is how sellers will be shown feedback

Viewing offers can also be accessed from both the step four section, or the navigation bar. This section will show any new offers that have been made on the property. You have the option to accept or reject and offers here, along with the opportunity to send a message along with your response. Any past offers will also show on this page, regardless of whether you rejected them, or if the buyer withdrew the offer.

Sellers will be able to respond to offers here
